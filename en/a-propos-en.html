Glossary

Direct Current (DC)

is a type of electrical flow where electrons move in a single, constant direction from one point to another. Unlike alternating current, direct current maintains a steady voltage over time. It is used in devices like batteries, power supplies, and some electronic devices.

Alternating Current (AC)

is a type of electrical flow where the direction of electron movement periodically reverses, alternating between one direction and the opposite. This type of current is used in modern power grids as it is more efficient for transmitting electricity over long distances. Household outlets typically provide alternating current.
