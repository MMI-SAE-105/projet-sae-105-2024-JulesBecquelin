Alfonso Gomez-Rejon: The Director Who Illuminated the War of Currents

Access the Wikipedia page

A Diverse and Daring Career

Born in 1972 in Laredo, Texas, Alfonso Gomez-Rejon began his career in the film industry as an assistant director for legends such as Martin Scorsese and Alejandro González Iñárritu. After gaining solid experience, he made his mark with his unique style in films like Me and Earl and the Dying Girl (2015), a moving drama that earned him numerous awards.

With The Current War, released in 2017 and re-released as a "Director's Cut" in 2019, he once again demonstrated his skill in combining technical storytelling with emotional depth. The film, initially delayed due to external circumstances, found its audience thanks to Gomez-Rejon's vision, who reinvented the editing to offer a version more faithful to his original vision.

A Striking Visual Vision

Gomez-Rejon is known for his ability to inject visual energy into his films, and The Current War is no exception. By portraying the rivalry between Edison, Tesla, and Westinghouse, he uses light as a true character in the film. Each scene is carefully lit to reflect the tensions, triumphs, and failures of the protagonists.

His dynamic and sometimes intimate camera choices plunge the viewer into the heart of this war of currents, allowing them to feel the urgency and importance of these discoveries that would transform the world. The attention to historical details, combined with a stylized visual approach, makes The Current War both educational and captivating.

A Director Attentive to Characters

Although The Current War is a film about electricity, Alfonso Gomez-Rejon puts the characters at the forefront. Rather than focusing solely on the technical aspects of the war of currents, he explores the human dimension of this rivalry. Thomas Edison, played by Benedict Cumberbatch, is portrayed as both brilliant and ruthless, while Nikola Tesla, played by Nicholas Hoult, is depicted as a genius ahead of his time but often misunderstood. Michael Shannon plays George Westinghouse with industrial pragmatism that contrasts with Tesla's more romantic ideals.

Gomez-Rejon delves into the deeper motivations of these characters, their strengths and weaknesses, making this struggle for technological supremacy as human as possible. It's this duality—between technology and humanity—that enriches the film.

The Art of Telling a Complex Story

Gomez-Rejon's ability to make a technical story accessible is one of his greatest strengths as a director. The Current War deals with highly complex subjects, but thanks to his fluid storytelling and sense of rhythm, the viewer is immersed in this race for the electrification of the world without ever getting lost in technical details. The tension builds progressively, keeping the focus on the human stakes behind each invention.

An Inspired Direction

As a director, Alfonso Gomez-Rejon demonstrates a unique ability to capture emotional stakes in historical narratives. His passion for history and technology is palpable in every frame of The Current War. He has transformed an episode of industrial history into a thrilling cinematic work while respecting the historical accuracy of the events.
